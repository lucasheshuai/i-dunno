# Rebuild Brief: Reshape Product Logic and Positioning for the Social Insights App

## Mission
Reshape the current product from a “question bank / polling app” into a more addictive, insight-driven, feed-like product.

The current issue is:
Users may enjoy the first 1–2 questions, but they do not feel enough motivation to continue answering many more. The current loop is too repetitive:
answer -> predict -> results -> next question

This needs to evolve into a product with:
- stronger curiosity
- stronger surprise
- stronger sense of personal progression
- stronger feeling that each next question may reveal something meaningful

Do NOT treat this as a simple UI polish task.
This is primarily a product logic and information architecture redesign.

---

# 1. New Product Positioning

## Previous positioning
A social question / prediction / results app.

## New positioning
A personalized social insights feed that helps users:
- discover how people really think
- find where they differ from the crowd
- test how well they read society
- gradually build a profile of their worldview

The product should feel closer to:
- a social insights feed
- a “read the crowd” game
- a reveal-based personality / perspective product

It should feel LESS like:
- a question bank
- a survey tool
- a static polling site

Important:
Users should not feel they are “answering 50 questions.”
They should feel they are moving through a stream of socially interesting reveals.

---

# 2. Core Product Problem to Solve

The current product lacks a compelling reason to continue after the first few questions because:
- the loop becomes predictable too quickly
- every question feels structurally similar
- there is no escalating reward
- there is no larger narrative or profile being built
- the “next question” does not feel exciting enough

The redesign should solve for:
1. stronger variable reward
2. stronger “next reveal” anticipation
3. stronger accumulation of personal insight
4. stronger topic continuity
5. stronger feed logic instead of question-bank logic

---

# 3. Replace “Question Bank” Logic with “Insight Feed” Logic

## Current problem
Users can sense there is just a list of many questions.
This creates fatigue and makes the experience feel finite and task-like.

## New direction
Hide the idea of a “50-question bank.”
The app should present questions as a curated stream / feed.

## Implementation instruction
- Remove or reduce any UI that exposes the full question inventory too early.
- Replace “browse all questions” as the main experience with a feed-based experience.
- Keep an explore page, but do not make it the core behavior.
- The core behavior should be: enter the feed -> answer -> get a reveal -> get teased into the next reveal.

Users should feel:
“what’s the next interesting reveal?”
not
“how many questions are left?”

---

# 4. Redesign the Core Loop

## Old loop
Question -> predict majority -> see chart -> next

## New loop
Question -> answer -> predict -> receive a variable insight reward -> see personalized interpretation -> see a teased next question -> continue

Each completed step should feel like:
- surprise
- interpretation
- anticipation

## Required loop outcome
After each answered question, the product must generate at least one of the following:
- “You were in the minority”
- “You misread the crowd”
- “Men and women split strongly here”
- “Your age group differs from the overall result”
- “This strengthens your profile as [type]”
- “This reveals a blind spot in how you read people”

Do NOT let every result screen feel identical.

---

# 5. Introduce Variable Reward Types

Create multiple types of reward cards / result modules so the app does not feel repetitive.

## Build the following reward module types

### A. Crowd Shock
Examples:
- You picked the minority view
- Only 19% agreed with you
- You were much more traditional than the crowd on this one

### B. Prediction Score
Examples:
- You guessed the crowd correctly
- You misread the crowd by 24 points
- You tend to overestimate how practical people are

### C. Demographic Split
Examples:
- Men and women disagreed sharply on this question
- Singles and married users saw this differently
- Your age group was more aligned with you than the full sample

### D. Profile Builder
Examples:
- This answer strengthens your “Social Realist” profile
- You consistently value stability over chemistry
- You read money-related attitudes well, but struggle with relationship norms

### E. Topic Hook
Examples:
- Next: what people actually mean when they say “marrying up”
- Next: the follow-up question where this topic gets more divided
- Next: a question people consistently misjudge

## Important implementation rule
Not every result screen needs every module.
Choose 1–3 strong modules dynamically so the product feels varied.

---

# 6. Create “Next Reveal” Hooks Instead of Generic “Next Question”

## Current problem
The CTA to continue is weak because “next question” sounds like more work.

## New direction
Every result page must tease the next item with a curiosity-based label.

## Replace generic CTAs like:
- Next question
- Continue

## With CTAs like:
- Next: the question men and women disagreed on most
- Next: a topic people often misread
- Next: a deeper follow-up on this issue
- Next: the one question that may change your profile most
- Next: how people define “settling” differently

## Implementation instruction
Build a metadata field for each question such as:
- teaserText
- followUpQuestionIds
- topicClusterId

Use these fields to generate continuity.

---

# 7. Organize Questions into Topic Clusters / Journeys

## Current problem
Questions feel isolated.

## New direction
Group questions into mini-journeys of 4–6 related questions.

Examples:
- Dating Standards
- Marriage & Status
- Gender Expectations
- Money in Relationships
- Career vs Love
- Modern Relationship Values

## Each cluster should:
- start with a broad question
- move into a more specific or revealing follow-up
- end with a mini-summary or cluster insight

## Example cluster structure
Topic Cluster: Marriage & Status
1. In the marriages you’ve seen recently, what pattern feels most common?
2. What matters most when people judge whether someone “married up”?
3. Who is more judged for marrying down?
4. Are women today more willing to marry someone with lower income than before?
5. What does “a good match” usually mean in modern marriage?

## Implementation instruction
Add a structure like:
- topicClusterId
- clusterOrder
- clusterTitle
- clusterIntro
- clusterOutro

After 4–5 questions in the same cluster, show a “cluster summary” screen.

---

# 8. Add Summary and Accumulation Mechanics

## Core idea
Users should feel they are building something by answering more questions.

## Build milestone-based unlocks

### After 3 answers
Unlock:
- Your first social reading score

### After 5 answers
Unlock:
- Your early perspective profile

### After 8 answers
Unlock:
- Where you differ most from the crowd

### After 12 answers
Unlock:
- Which category you read best
- Which category you consistently misjudge

### After 15+ answers
Unlock:
- Your strongest disagreement topic
- Your biggest blind spot
- Your most aligned demographic group

## Important
Do not make this feel like cheap gamification.
This is not about points for the sake of points.
This is about accumulating insight.

---

# 9. Build a Persistent Personal Profile

Create a lightweight but evolving user profile page.

## The profile should show
- Questions answered
- Crowd prediction accuracy
- Top categories answered
- Perspective labels
- Biggest disagreement topic
- Most aligned group
- Most common answer pattern

## Example profile labels
- Social Realist
- Crowd Reader
- Modern Pragmatist
- Romantic Skeptic
- Value-First Thinker
- Stability Seeker

These can be simple heuristic rules in V1.

## Important
The profile must evolve visibly as the user answers more.
It should feel like the app is “learning” the user.

---

# 10. Reduce Friction and Make the Flow Feel More Feed-Like

Borrow the psychology of TikTok / short-form feeds, but do NOT literally turn it into a short video app.

## The product should borrow:
- low friction
- “maybe the next one will surprise me” logic
- fast progression
- variable reward
- personalization

## The product should NOT become:
- random endless question spam
- low-quality viral bait
- dark-pattern addiction product

## Implementation instruction
- Keep one question per screen
- Make transitions fast and smooth
- Minimize clicks between answer and reveal
- Avoid showing large inventories or menus during the main flow
- Reduce visual clutter between stages
- Make each completion feel fast and satisfying

---

# 11. Rework the Home Screen

## Current home should no longer feel like a static landing page for a survey product

## New home screen should emphasize:
- your personal insight progress
- where you left off
- a strong “continue your feed” CTA
- one or two compelling insight teasers
- your current profile status
- optionally: a “today’s most divisive topic” card

## Suggested homepage blocks
1. Main CTA: Continue Your Feed
2. Current profile snapshot
3. Your latest reveal / surprise
4. Featured topic cluster
5. One high-curiosity entry point

Example CTA labels:
- Continue your insight feed
- Pick up where you left off
- See your next reveal
- Continue reading the crowd

---

# 12. Rework the Explore Page

The explore page should remain available, but should no longer feel like a giant question bank.

## Instead, redesign explore around:
- topic clusters
- trending social tensions
- “most divisive”
- “most misread”
- “best for your profile”
- “continue this thread”

## Example explore sections
- Trending Divides
- Marriage & Status
- Career vs Relationships
- Topics You May Care About
- Questions People Misjudge Most

Avoid showing a plain list of 50 questions.
